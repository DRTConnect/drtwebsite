About This Historical Sanctuary

Biak na Bato National Park is a 2,117-hectare protected area combining natural wonders with revolutionary history. Known as the mountain fortress of Filipino revolutionaries in 1897, the park features limestone caves, crystal-clear rivers, and dense forests that served as natural bunkers during the Philippine Revolution.

The park's name translates to "Split Stone" from Tagalog, referring to its unique geological formations. Explore the Aguinaldo Cave where the historic Pact of Biak-na-Bato was signed, and wade through the Madlum River's emerald waters flowing through marble canyons.

Declared a national park in 1937, this ecological and historical treasure offers both adventure and lessons in Philippine heritage.
