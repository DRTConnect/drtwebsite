About The Cascading Beauty

Tibagan Falls features a series of seven natural pools cascading down moss-covered rock formations. The main waterfall drops 10 meters into a jade-green basin, surrounded by century-old narra trees and wild orchids. Known for its "stairway to paradise" formation, each tier offers unique swimming experiences.

The waters originate from underground springs, maintaining a cool 20¬∞C temperature year-round. Local legends say the falls were named after the Tagalog word "tibag" (to dig), referring to hidden chambers used during the Japanese occupation.

First developed for tourism in 2016, Tibagan remains one of DRT's most photogenic natural attractions with minimal commercial development.
