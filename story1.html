Living Culture in the Green Heart of Bulacan

The Dumagat are among the original settlers of the Sierra Madre region, including areas within DRT. Traditionally semi-nomadic, they have long relied on nature for food, shelter, and medicine, practicing age-old methods of hunting, gathering, and forest farming. Despite the modern world's pressures, many Dumagat families still preserve their ancestral knowledge, rituals, and language‚Äîmaking them living links to the country's pre-colonial heritage.

Their presence in DRT is not just a matter of history; it is a vibrant part of the community's identity today. Whether as forest guides, storytellers, or craft makers, the Dumagat are actively engaged in sharing their wisdom with visitors through eco-tours, cultural programs, and community experiences.

Recognized by the Department of Tourism

In 2023, as part of its 50th Anniversary celebration, the Department of Tourism (DOT) officially recognized and accredited indigenous cultural performers and tourism partners‚Äîincluding the Dumagat Tribe. This accreditation acknowledges their invaluable contributions to heritage conservation and sustainable tourism development.

With training and support from the DOT and other partners, some Dumagat members have become licensed cultural guides and accredited tourism providers, helping bridge the gap between their traditions and the world of modern travel. Their inclusion in national tourism efforts represents a strong step toward empowering indigenous voices in tourism‚Äîwhere they are no longer merely part of the attraction, but respected partners and storytellers of their own narratives.
